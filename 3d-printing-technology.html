Christopher Barnatt

Christopher Barnatt was a professional futurist for more than 20 years, also runs ExplainingTheFuture.com, ExplainingComputers.com along with also their affiliated YouTube stations. He has published twelve books and numerous articles, together with over 200 contributions.

Brian Evans

Brian Evans is an artist working on digital media and Assistant Professor of Art at Metropolitan State University of Denver, where he teaches multidisciplinary classes in artwork on topics which have electronics and electronic fabrication. Many of the classes use open up hardware--including MakerBot or RepRap 3D printers and the Arduino electronics platform--to create new works in art and design.

He is the author of Practical 3D Printers (Apress, 2012), Beginning Arduino Programming (Apress, 2011), and contributed to Arduino Projects to Save the World (Apress, 2011) with Emery Premeaux. His work has been shown in the Los Angeles Municipal Art Gallery at Barnsdall Park, the Orange County Center for Contemporary Art, and the University Art Museum at California State University, Long Beach. In 2009, Evans was a resident and also contributor to the Grounding Open Source Hardware residency and summit in the Banff New Media Institute in Alberta, Canada, and at 2011 a contributor to the Open Hardware Summit in New York. He also received an MFA from California State University, Long Beach in 2008, and a BFA from Arizona State University in 2005.
